STAR & DELTA Systems

Interactive Guide to Three-Phase Electrical Connections

What is Star Connection?

In a Star (or Y) connection, one end of each of the three phase windings is connected together at a common point called the Neutral Point (N). The other ends are connected to the three line wires (R, Y, B).

This setup allows for two different voltage levels: Phase Voltage (between line and neutral) and Line Voltage (between any two lines).

💡
Common Use: Household distribution. The 230V in your wall socket is the Phase Voltage, while the 400V across lines is for heavy industrial use.
Key Relationships
Line Voltage: VL = √3 × Vph
Line Current: IL = Iph

Visual Diagram

Derivation: Why VL = √3 × Vph ?

1

Phase Voltages

In a balanced system, the three phase voltages are equal in magnitude but separated by 120 degrees.

VR = Vph ∠0°
VY = Vph ∠−120°
VB = Vph ∠−240°
2

Vector Subtraction

Line voltage is the potential difference between two lines (e.g., VRY). By Kirchhoff's Voltage Law, going from Y to R through the neutral:

VRY = VR − VY = Vph ∠0° − Vph ∠−120°
3

Trigonometric Expansion

Using rectangular coordinates (cos θ + j sin θ):

VRY = Vph[1 - (-0.5 - j0.866)]
VRY = Vph(1.5 + j0.866)

Magnitude |VRY| = Vph × √(1.5² + 0.866²) = Vph × √(2.25 + 0.75) = Vph × √3.

What is Delta Connection?

In a Delta (or Δ) connection, the end of one phase winding connects to the start of the next, forming a closed triangle loop. The line wires connect at the three corners.

There is no neutral wire in this setup. The voltage across any winding is directly equal to the voltage between the two lines connected to it.

🔥
Common Use: High-power industrial motors and power transmission, where high current and starting torque are essential.
Key Relationships
Line Voltage: VL = Vph
Line Current: IL = √3 × Iph

Visual Diagram

Derivation: Why IL = √3 × Iph ?

1

Kirchhoff's Current Law (KCL)

At any node (corner of the delta), the current entering from the line must equal the difference between the phase currents connecting to that node.

ILine(R) = IRY − IBR
2

Phase Currents Vector

The phase currents inside the delta are separated by 120°.

IRY = Iph ∠0°
IBR = Iph ∠−240° (or +120°)

Subtracting them gives |ILine| = √3 × Iph (by the same vector math shown in the Star voltage derivation).

Head-to-Head Comparison

A quick reference table summarizing the key differences between Star and Delta configurations.

Property ⭐ Star (Y) △ Delta (Δ)
Line Voltage (VL) √3 × Vph = Vph
Line Current (IL) = Iph √3 × Iph
Neutral Wire ✅ Available ❌ Not available
Phase Voltage Lower (VL / √3) Higher (= VL)
Total Power Same! P = √3 · VL · IL · cosφ
Insulation Requirement Less (due to lower Vph) More (due to higher Vph)
Common Uses Distribution, Lighting, Homes Motors, High-Power Transformers

Analogy: Star

🚲
Like a bicycle wheel: all spokes (phases) meet at a central hub (neutral). You can measure from any spoke to the hub independently.

Analogy: Delta

🔺
Like a triangular pipe loop: flow continuously moves corner to corner. Great for pushing heavy loads without needing a center drain.
